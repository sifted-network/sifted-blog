arXiv:2605.21724v1 Announce Type: cross Abstract: Hyper-Connections (HC) improve residual networks by introducing learnable mixing across multiple residual streams, but unconstrained mixing leads to training instability. Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC) address this by enforcing approximate double stochasticity via Sinkhorn normalization, while mHC-lite ensures exact constraints through convex combinations of permutation matrices at the cost of factorial complexity. KromHC reduces this cost using Kronecker-product parameterizations, but restricts the mixing matrices to a structured submanifold of the Birkhoff polytope . We propose Transportation Birkhoff Polytope (TBP) parameterizations and their Recursive variants (RTBP), which construct exactly doubly stochastic mixing matrices with $(n-1)^2$ degrees of freedom. Our approach avoids iterative normalization and combinatorial explosion while preserving full expressivity of the Birkhoff polytope. Empirical results on language model pre-training' demonstrate competitive performance with improved stability and scalability.
